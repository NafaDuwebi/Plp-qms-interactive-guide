What is the QMS?

A system of processes, procedures, documents and responsibilities that ensure PLP delivers high-quality services consistently. Maintaining it earns ISO 9001:2015 accreditation — a recognised standard that guarantees quality to clients.

People & accountability

Roles & responsibilities

Everyone at PLP has a part to play in the QMS. Your role determines what you're responsible for maintaining, improving and evidencing.

QM

Quality Manager

Owns and maintains the QMS overall

Maintains the Process Architecture on SharePoint

Coordinates all internal and external audits

Manages process documentation and approvals

Maintains the Active Improvement Log

Reports QMS status to the Management Team

PO

Process Owner

Responsible for their assigned process

Defines and maintains the process procedure

Ensures staff follow the written procedure

Agrees and manages corrective actions

Conducts spot checks of process deliverables

Acts as contact for external auditors

AU

Auditor

Independent reviewer of a process

Checks work matches the documented procedure

Records conformities and non-conformities

Produces the audit report with findings

Agrees findings with the process owner

Must be independent of the audited process

Additional roles
DIR

Director

Reviews and signs off all major process changes and new documents. Authorises the Process Architecture structure and receives regular QMS status updates from the QM.

ALL

All staff

Every member of staff is expected to follow the procedures for their role, suggest improvements when they identify them, and cooperate fully with auditors when a process is being reviewed.

Internal audit programme

The internal audit cycle

Internal audits check that work is being carried out in line with documented procedures and identify improvements. All 25 processes are audited on a rolling schedule managed by the Quality Manager.

1

Plan

QM schedules audits by priority. Assigns an auditor and audit owner. Records on the Internal Quality Audit Schedule.

2

Initiate

QM prepares the report template. Audit owner agrees scope and confirms authority to proceed.

3

Conduct

Auditor interviews process owner, checks evidence against the procedure, records conformities and non-conformities.

4

Report

Auditor agrees findings with process owner. Final report sent to audit owner and QM.

5

Resolve

QM logs non-conformities and improvements in the Improvement Log. Actions agreed and tracked to closure.

Scoring & findings

Conformity · 1/1

Evidence found that the process step is being followed as documented. Note where the evidence was seen (e.g., SharePoint, CRM, meeting minutes).

Non-conformity · 0/1

A process step is not being followed, or evidence cannot be found. The step must be scored 0/1 and recorded with a corrective action.

Improvement

A suggestion to improve the process or its wording — even if the step is currently conformant. Recorded separately from non-conformities.

📋

Scoring rule

If any non-conformity is found within a step, the entire step must be scored 0/1. Once all steps are scored the overall percentage conformity is calculated. This feeds directly into the Internal Audit Overview Report.
